An employer may dismiss an employee for the following reasons:

  • Economic hardship
  • Downsizing or plant closure
  • Permanent illness
  • or loss of work permit, Repeated tardiness, Refusal to work or Theft

Additionally, there are other reasons that might justify dismissal, such as gross misconduct, violation of company policies, harassment or violence in the workplace, and inability to get along with coworkers. Employers are required to follow legal and contractual obligations when dismissing an employee, and the specific reasons and procedures can vary depending on local labor laws and individual employment contracts (vgi. Entscheidungen...).
